Early Life

On October 29th, 1942, Robert Norman Ross was born in Daytona, Florida. Shortly after, he and his family moved to Orlando, where he was raised. Bob dropped out after his first year of high school, and worked part time as a carpenter with his father. At age 18, he enlisted in the US Air Force. During his time stationed in Alaska, he joined the United Service Organizations club around some time in the 1960’s. After joining the club, he discovered his desire for painting. Bob especially enjoyed oil painting, and made some money from selling portraits of the Alaskan mountains. Once he retired from the military in 1981, he took private painting lessons.

Career

After Bob’s retirement, he fell in love with painting. He got private lessons with Bill Alexander, and shortly replaced him on his PBS show. “The Joy of Painting” premiered in 1983 and ran for 11 years. Fans adored Ross’s lovable hippie vibe, and his smooth speaking voice. His viewers were blown away by his careless painting methods that were easy and pleasing to look at. Bob marketed his technique and establish a successful company named Bob Ross Incorporated. He sold books, videos, art supplies, and workshops. On July 4th, 1995, Bob Ross sadly passed away due to Lymphoma. Despite his death, Bob Ross has made a positive impact on the world. He brought people together who shared a common joy, painting.
